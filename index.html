Data provenance & cohort cascade. Of 1,24,214 covered lives, 50,928 registered and completed blood diagnostics, and 43,916 activated — the tracked clinical cohort, all with a full blood panel + onboarding vitals + PHQ-9 / GAD-7 screening; 29,863 (68%) have ≥2 assessments for matched re-screen. All clinical metrics are computed on these 43,916 activated members. Subgroup denominators: 22,040 dysglycaemic (HbA1c ≥5.7 — 9,204 diabetic, 12,836 prediabetic); 25,552 dyslipidaemic; 19,910 elevated HSI (MAFLD); 18,453 on ≥1 metabolic medication; 16,409 obese (BMI ≥30); 14,632 hypertensive (BP ≥130/85); 8,317 anaemic; 6,271 renal-risk. Standard scoring: PHQ-9 / GAD-7 bands (Minimal 0–4, Mild 5–9, Moderate 10–14, Mod-severe 15–19, Severe 20+). Diabetes remission defined as HbA1c <6.5% off glucose-lowering medication, sustained ≥3 months. The Registration & Activation funnel at the top is the source population — the clinical sections detail the 43,916 activated members within it.

Reference thresholds shown on the result bars are drawn from the Twin Health Whole Body Digital Twin™ India RCT outcomes (Joshi S, Shamanna P, et al., Endocr Pract. 2023;29(12):960–970; 18-month follow-up Diabetes 2024) and Kaiser Permanente real-world results. Cohort results are real-world and typically track slightly below controlled-trial outcomes.
